Reuben Sandwich

Back HOME Next

Description

A Reuben is a grilled sandwich featuring corned beef on rye bread. The Reuben is a menu staple in Jewish-style deli-
catessens, but it's not technically kosher because it combines meat and cheese.

Ingredients

  • 8 slices rye bread
  • ½ cup Thousand Island dressing
  • 8 slices Swiss cheese
  • 8 slices deli sliced corned beef
  • 1 cup sauerkraut, drained
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened

Steps

You'll find the full, step-by-step recipe below — but here's a brief overview of what you can expect when you make this simple reuben sandwich:

  1. Preheat a large griddle or skillet over medium heat.
  2. Spread one side of bread slices evenly with Thousand
    Island dressing. On four bread slices, layer one slice
    Swiss cheese, 2 slices corned beef, 1/4 cup sauerkraut,
    and a second slice of Swiss cheese. Top with remaining
    bread slices, dressing-side down. Butter the top of each sandwich.
  3. Place sandwiches, butter-side down on the preheated
    griddle; butter the top of each sandwich with remaining butter.
    Grill until both sides are golden brown, about 5 minutes per side. Serve hot.
